Features

Highlights

The seamless, one-piece light strip including ‘PORSCHE’ logo at the rear connects the three-dimensional LED taillights.

Wheels

The 911 Turbo is fitted with 20/21-inch 911 Turbo forged alloy wheels. The wheels have a titanium-coloured two-tone finish and the wheel centres feature a Porsche Crest.

Roof

It goes without saying that the air supply plays a decisive role in a 911 Turbo. But why limit it to the engine? The 911 Turbo Cabriolet models unite the classic silhouette of the 911

Seats

The fully electric Sports seats (14-way) are not only comfortable, but also provide good lateral support. Seat height and backrest adjustment can be adjusted electrically

Porsche 911 Turbo S

Especifications

Performance

1

Motor

In the 911 Turbo, the unit delivers 427kW (580PS), and 478kW (650PS) in the 911 Turbo S. The sprint performances are excellent. The 911 Turbo reaches 100km/h in 2.8 seconds

2

Air Cooling

Charge-air cooling and engine air intake have been completely redesigned. Previously, air for the intercooler was supplied via side air intakes in the rear wings and the aspirated engine air via a tailgate grille

3

Emissions

Fuel consumption combined: 12,3 – 12,0 l/100 km CO2-emissions combined (WLTP): 278 – 271 g/km CO2 Class: G

Design

4

Chassis

PASM is an electronic damping control system that actively and continuously adjusts the damp- ing force on each wheel, based on current road conditions and driving style - for reduced body roll and thus for improved comfort and increased dynamics.

5

Brakes

On the 911 Turbo models, the brakes feature a reduction in unsprung masses and therefore better roadholding and increased comfort, particularly on uneven roads, as well as greater agility and a further improvement in handling

$6

Aerodynamics

You cannot avoid the wind. But you can direct it. The aerodynamic system, which has been enhanced compared with the previous generation, makes the 911 Turbo models even more aerodynamically flexible in any situation. PAA ensures optimum performance, along with fuel consumption and CO₂ emissions benefits.
